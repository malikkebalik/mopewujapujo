How Much is K. Michelle Worth?


The estimated net worth of Kimberly Michelle Pate is $1.5 Million

A Closer Look at Kimberly Michelle Pate

Kimberly Michelle Pate, more commonly known as K. Michelle, is a well-known American singer, songwriter, and record producer. She gained recognition in 2009 with the release of her single “Fakin’ It,” featuring vocals from Missy Elliott. The song peaked at #100 on the Billboard Hot R&B/Hip-Hop Songs chart and received positive reviews, leading to the subsequent success of her other singles. This ultimately led to K. Michelle signing a record deal with Atlanta Records and releasing her debut album, “Rebellious Soul,” in 2013, which was well-received and spawned hit singles such as “V. S. O. P” and “Can’t Raise a Man.”

K. Michelle’s Net Worth: $1.5 Million

So, how wealthy is K. Michelle? In 2012, she earned $50,000 for her first season on “Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta,” and her salary for the second season increased to $200,000. Additionally, K. Michelle signed a $3 million contract with Atlanta Records and earned $512,000 from the sales of her “Rebellious Soul” album in 2013. Her overall net worth is estimated at $1.5 million, predominantly amassed through her music career and television appearances.

K. Michelle was born in 1984 in Tennessee, USA, and showed a passion for music from a young age. She received piano and guitar lessons and pursued a double degree in music and psychology from Florida A&M University. K. Michelle’s musical journey began in 2009 when she signed with Jive Record and released “Fakin’ It,” garnering significant attention. Despite leaving the label before her debut album’s planned release, K. Michelle’s career continued to flourish, leading to her involvement in “Love & Hip Hop: Atlanta” and her subsequent signing with Atlanta Records in 2012. Following her debut album, she released her second studio album, “Anybody Wanna Buy a Heart?,” in 2014, featuring popular singles such as “Maybe I Should Call” and “Love ‘Em All.”

In her personal life, K. Michelle was in a relationship with basketball player Lance Stephenson before dating Bobby Maze, a former college basketball player.
