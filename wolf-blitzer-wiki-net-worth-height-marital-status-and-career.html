Age and Early Life of Wolf Blitzer

On March 22, 1948, Wolf was born in Augsburg, Germany. He is 74 years old as well. He was born to Cesia and David Blitzer during the Allied occupation that followed World War II. His mother was a stay-at-home mom, while his father built houses.

His parents were Auschwitz concentration camp survivors from Nazi-occupied Poland. All of his relatives perished at the camp. The 1948 Displaced Persons Act allowed Blitzer and his family to move to the United States.

Blitzer was raised in Buffalo, New York, where he was born. He completed his high school education at Kenmore West Senior High School. He earned a bachelor’s degree in history from the State University of New York at Buffalo in 1970.

Wolf earned a master’s degree in international affairs from the School of Advanced International Studies at Johns Hopkins University in 1972. He studied Hebrew at the Hebrew University of Jerusalem while attending Johns Hopkins.

Professional Career

Early in the 1970s, Wolf began his journalism career. He was employed at the Tel Aviv bureau of the Reuters news agency. Wolf caught the attention of Jerusalem Post editor Ari Rath and was hired as a Washington correspondent for an English-language Israeli daily.

He stayed on staff at the company until 1990. During his time there, he covered both the Middle East and American political issues.

Because he spoke Hebrew well, he also used to contribute articles to newspapers in that language.

Wolf employed a number of pen names when writing for Hebrew-language publications. Wolf served as the editor of the American Israel Public Affairs Committee’s monthly publication, the Near East Report, in the middle of the 1970s (AIPAC).

In articles for AIPAC, Wolf primarily discussed Middle East topics in relation to US foreign policy.
